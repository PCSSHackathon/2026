The Sci-Tech Program.

Port Credit Secondary School's Regional STEM pathway — open to all Peel students, launched in 2005, and still going strong.

ABOUT THE PROGRAM

A Regional Program.
Open to All of Peel.

The Science & Technology (Sci-Tech) Program at Port Credit Secondary School is a four-year Regional Learning Choice Program (RLCP) launched in 2005. Because it's a regional program, no flex boundary forms are required — any student across the Peel District School Board can apply.

The program follows an integrated curriculum of science and technology from Grade 9 through 12, with technology woven deeper into each year. Students learn through a hands-on, minds-on philosophy — working with real equipment, real tools, and real problems from day one.

What makes it different.

Four pillars that define every year of the program.

🔬

Hands-On Learning

Every concept gets applied. Students work with real lab equipment, build real circuits, and write real code — not just read about how things work.

💻

Coding & Engineering

From programming fundamentals to embedded systems and robotics, students build technical skills that directly transfer to post-secondary programs and careers.

🤝

Industry & Co-op Links

Sci-Tech partners with post-secondary institutions and industry to give students co-op placements, apprenticeship pathways, and real-world exposure beyond the classroom.

🏆

Competitions & Events

From in-school hackathons like this one to regional science fairs, robotics competitions, and showcases, Sci-Tech students represent PCSS at the highest level each year.

Courses in the pathway.

All Sci-Tech students take the regional (R) version of courses where available, giving them a deeper, more integrated experience in each subject.

Gr. Core Courses What You'll Focus On
9 Intro to Computer Science · Science (SNC1W) · Math Programming basics, scientific method, lab safety, and building the foundation for all four years.
10 ICS2O · Chemistry (SCH2D) · Physics · Science Data types, algorithms, hands-on chemistry labs, and introductory hardware concepts.
11 ICS3U · Physics (SPH3U) · Functions · Biology Object-oriented programming, embedded systems, and applying math to real engineering problems.
12 ICS4U · Computer Engineering (TEJ4M) · Calculus · Capstone Advanced programming, microcontroller projects, independent research, and preparing for post-secondary STEM.

A Manufacturing SHSM (Specialist High Skills Major) option is also available for students in Grades 11–12, combining Sci-Tech coursework with industry certifications and co-op placements.

WHERE GRADUATES GO

After PCSS Sci-Tech.

Sci-Tech graduates have gone on to study Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, Mechatronics, and Software Development at universities and colleges across Ontario — including Waterloo, U of T, McMaster, Western, and Sheridan.

Others have taken the applied path through Ontario Youth Apprenticeship (OYAP) or Dual Credit programs offered right at PCSS — earning college credits before they even graduate high school.

Common Questions.

Everything you need to know before the big day.

Registration

Who can attend?

Middle School students (Grades 6–8) interested in technology, coding, and engineering from any local middle school.

Technical

Do I need prior coding experience?

Absolutely not. Every track is built from the ground up for beginners. Mentors provide all tools, code, and step-by-step guidance throughout the day.

Registration

How much does it cost?

The PCSS Hackathon is completely free — technology, hardware kits, instruction, and lunch all included. No catches.

On The Day

What hardware do I need to bring?

Nothing! PCSS provides all lab computers, library laptops, and Arduino kits. Just bring yourself and a willingness to learn.

On The Day

Is food provided?

Yes! Pizza lunch and drinks are served midday. List any dietary restrictions clearly on your registration form.

Technical

Do I need specific accounts?

Gemini AI track needs a Google account. Minecraft track needs a school-issued Minecraft Education account. Everything else is provided on the day.

On The Day

Are parents allowed to stay?

Parents drop off between 9:45–10:00 AM and are encouraged to return at 1:10 PM for the closing ceremony where winners are announced and certificates handed out!

Teams

How do teams work?

Work solo or in small groups! Come with friends or alone — mentors will pair you with others in your track. Cross-school teams are totally encouraged.

Teams

Can students from different schools team up?

Absolutely! We love cross-school collaboration. Teaming up with new people is one of the most rewarding parts of the whole day.
